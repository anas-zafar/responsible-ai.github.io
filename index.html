Abstract

As generative artificial intelligence (AI) systems become increasingly sophisticated and widespread, the question of responsibility becomes paramount. This comprehensive survey examines the complex landscape of accountability in generative AI, addressing the fundamental question: Who is responsible when AI systems cause harm or make problematic decisions?

We systematically analyze responsibility across four key dimensions: data (quality, bias, and provenance), models (architecture, training methodologies, and deployment decisions), users (application, misuse, and ethical considerations), and regulations (policy frameworks, governance structures, and enforcement mechanisms). Our analysis reveals that responsibility is not singular but distributed across this ecosystem of stakeholders.

Through extensive literature review and case study analysis, we identify critical gaps in current approaches to AI responsibility and propose a framework for sustainable and responsible generative AI development. Our findings emphasize the need for collaborative governance models that balance innovation with ethical considerations, ensuring that generative AI systems contribute positively to society while minimizing potential harms.

Survey Overview

This survey provides a comprehensive analysis of responsibility in generative AI systems, examining key stakeholders and their roles in ensuring ethical and sustainable AI development.

Key Contributions

  • Comprehensive Framework: A systematic analysis of responsibility across data, models, users, and regulations in generative AI systems.
  • Stakeholder Analysis: Detailed examination of roles and accountabilities for each key stakeholder in the AI ecosystem.
  • Gap Identification: Critical assessment of current approaches and identification of areas requiring further attention.
  • Sustainable AI Guidelines: Practical recommendations for developing responsible and sustainable generative AI systems.

Related Work

This survey builds upon extensive research in AI ethics, algorithmic fairness, and responsible AI development.

Key areas of related work include AI governance frameworks, algorithmic bias detection and mitigation, ethical AI design principles, and regulatory approaches to AI oversight.

Our work synthesizes insights from computer science, law, policy, and ethics to provide a holistic view of responsibility in the generative AI landscape.
